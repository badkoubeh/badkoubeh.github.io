I'm Babak Badkoubeh, a control and RL engineer. My research interest is a robustness benchmark for physical AI systems — because physical AI has no room for hallucination the way LLM systems do — and building scalable, robust RL agents that hold up under long-tail events. I believe reinforcement learning and world models are how we get there: LLMs alone won't reach AGI, but RL agents that learn and plan inside world models, in collaboration with LLMs, are a real path toward true intelligence. And before any of it touches the real world, one thing has to be true — it has to be safe, and you have to be able to prove it.

Mission

A framework to measure whether physical AI is robust, safe, and ethical.

Physical AI is the next leap — intelligence that has to move, decide, and act in the real world, not just answer. Unlike an LLM, it gets no room for hallucination: a wrong action meets reality and reality doesn't grade on a curve. My mission is a framework that measures the robustness, safety, and trustworthiness of these agents — turning "it works in the demo" into evidence you can actually stand behind, with the rigor we already demand of systems that carry human lives.

The insight that ties it together is the long-tail event — the rare moment that decides everything. Under that pressure, three questions collapse into one: did the agent stay stable, did it stay safe, and did it take an ethical action? They are not three separate tests. An agent that stays composed when the unexpected hits is the same agent that chooses a safe, defensible action instead of a catastrophic one. So I measure all three with one lens — robustness — and I build the robust RL agents that pass it, grounding their reasoning so that even at the edge of what they were trained on, they act in ways we can trust and inspect.

Vision

Ground trust in the real world — the laws of nature and beyond.

My vision is to ground this measurement in real-world models, not clean simulations or human-preference scores alone. Trust in physical AI has to be earned against reality — the laws of nature, real dynamics, real disturbances, and the messy regularities of the world that lie beyond any single equation. That grounding is what turns a benchmark into a gold-standard robustness benchmark the whole field can rely on, across robotics, autonomous vehicles, drones, and spacecraft — to physical AI what ImageNet was to vision. The framework stress-tests anything — classical controllers, robust RL policies, and adversarial RL agents that hunt for the disturbance that breaks a system — and treats world models as first-class subjects of evaluation, scoring whether their rollouts stay faithful to reality under pressure.

The longer arc is agents that don't just survive the real world but reason about it: grounding their decisions in physical and real-world models, extending learned principles into new hypotheses, and testing those hypotheses against reality — replacing blind exploration with structured, inspectable discovery. That is the kind of intelligence worth trusting with consequential action: one whose reasoning we can follow, measure, and hold to a standard.
